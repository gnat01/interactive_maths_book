## V1 — 4th Grade Core (The First Six Cases)

### Case 1 · The Forger's Code
**Topic: Multi-digit Multiplication**

| | |
|---|---|
| Hook | A smuggler encodes messages using multiplication keys. Crack the cipher. |
| Prerequisites | Times tables (not drilled — reinforced contextually throughout this case) |
| Unlocks | Long division, factors & multiples |

Key concepts:
- 2-digit × 2-digit (e.g. 34 × 27)
- 3-digit × 1-digit
- 3-digit × 2-digit
- Multiplication as a pattern, not memorisation
- Estimation and reasonableness checks
- Estimations that leverage "nice" numbers - for example, if given 49 * 99, it is nice for her to realise this is slightly less than 50 * 100 and so 5000 is an upper bound
- Comparison problems - which ia larger? 40 * 59 or 50 * 49, etc.
- Distributive properties to do multiplications fast : 12 * 11 = 12 * (10 + 1) ; encourages looking for shortcuts that make life easier

---

### Case 2 · The Missing Inheritance
**Topic: Long Division + Introduction to Fractions**

| | |
|---|---|
| Hook | An estate must be divided among heirs. Someone is fiddling the numbers. |
| Prerequisites | Case 1 (multiplication) |
| Unlocks | Equivalent fractions, decimals, probability |

Key concepts:
- Long division (3–4 digit ÷ 1-digit, then ÷ 2-digit)
- Remainders — and what they mean
- Remainder expressed as a fraction
- Equivalent fractions (½ = 2/4 = 4/8)
- Comparing and ordering fractions
- Simplifying fractions
- Fraction comparison via word puzzles - pirates make off with some fraction of jewels. Who made off with the most? 
---

### Case 3 · The Ghost Equation
**Topic: Introduction to Algebra**

| | |
|---|---|
| Hook | A message arrived with numbers erased. The unknown is marked with a symbol. Find it. |
| Prerequisites | All four operations (Cases 1 & 2) |
| Unlocks | Two-step equations, writing expressions, 5th grade algebra |

Key concepts:
- What is a variable? (x as "the hidden number in the code")
- Writing expressions from word problems
- Solving one-step equations (x + 5 = 12, 3x = 18)
- Solving two-step equations (2x + 3 = 11)
- Checking solutions ("does this decode the message correctly?")
- Inequalities — introduction (x > 5 means "the code is larger than 5")
- Finding a hidden number from constraints "I am a number between 0 to 100. I am even and 4 does not divide me." - now as she guesses, give other math statements to help her narrow down to the answer
- Finding secret codes using positional clues. "The safe in a castle has a 4 digit code. The tens place is 3 times the thousands place and the 1s place is 4 less than the hundreds place. What possible choices are there for the code?"

### Case 4 · The Architect's Secret
**Topic: Geometry — Shapes, Angles, Area**

| | |
|---|---|
| Hook | A stolen blueprint contains hidden rooms. Deduce their dimensions from the clues. |
| Prerequisites | Basic shape recognition (assumed), multiplication (Case 1) |
| Unlocks | Coordinate geometry, volume, circles (advanced) |

Key concepts:
- Angles — types (acute, right, obtuse, reflex), measuring with a protractor
- Triangles — types (equilateral, isosceles, scalene, right-angled), angle sum = 180°
- Quadrilaterals — square, rectangle, parallelogram, rhombus, trapezium — properties and distinctions
- Circles — radius, diameter, circumference (concept), relationship between radius and diameter
- Perimeter of polygons
- Area — rectangles, triangles (½ × base × height)
- Symmetry — lines of symmetry, rotational symmetry

---

### Case 5 · The Census Conspiracy
**Topic: Statistics & Data**

| | |
|---|---|
| Hook | A town's census records have been tampered with. Find the anomalies in the data. |
| Prerequisites | All four operations, fractions (Case 2) |
| Unlocks | Probability (Case 6), more advanced statistics (V2) |

Key concepts:
- Mean, median, mode, range — what each tells you and when to use which
- Reading and interpreting bar charts, line graphs, pictograms
- Drawing charts from raw data
- Spotting misleading data / outliers
- Frequency tables
- Comparing two datasets

---

### Case 6 · The Probability Heist
**Topic: Introduction to Probability**

| | |
|---|---|
| Hook | A crew is planning a heist that depends on dice rolls going their way. What are the real odds? |
| Prerequisites | Fractions (Case 2), statistics (Case 5) |
| Unlocks | Backgammon Lab (V2), combined events, experimental probability |

Key concepts:
- Probability as a fraction, decimal, and percentage
- The probability scale (0 = impossible → 1 = certain)
- Listing all possible outcomes (sample space)
- Theoretical vs experimental probability
- Simple probability calculations (single events)
- Fairness and bias
- Probability in everyday language ("likely", "unlikely", "evens")
- Conditional probability basics : the first die showed 5, what is the probability that the second shows 5? - The first die showed 4, what is the probability that the sum of the two dice is more than 8?
- A small small whiff of where frequentist thinking is not possible -- maybe introduce her to probabilities of things like a volcano eruption and say "How can we do this by counting? Does counting evenr matter? Etc"

## V1 Cold Cases (Optional Digressions)

These unlock mid-way through V1 for students moving fast. They are harder, self-contained, and do not block the main narrative.

- **The Infinite Mirror** — introduction to patterns, sequences, and simple series (foreshadows 5th grade)
- **The Broken Scale** — logic puzzles using balance equations (deepens algebra intuition)
- **The Forger Returns** — harder cipher problems combining multiplication, division, and basic algebra
- **The Surveyor's Riddle** — composite area problems (combining rectangles and triangles in one figure)

## V2 — 4th Grade Completion + Backgammon Lab

### The Backgammon Lab
An interactive board where she can:
- Set up any position and calculate "how many rolls get me out of here?"
- See probability distributions for dice combinations (e.g. what is P(rolling a 6 with 2 dice)?)
- Replay positions from real games and analyse decisions (STRETCH, NOT NECESSARY FOR V2)
- Run simulated games to test strategies (STRETCH, NOT NEEDED FOR V2)

Prerequisite: Case 6 (probability foundations must be solid first)

### Additional V2 Topics

**Fractions — going deeper**
- Adding and subtracting fractions with unlike denominators
- Multiplying fractions (½ × ¾)
- Dividing fractions (conceptual introduction)
- Mixed numbers and improper fractions
- Fractions on a number line

Comment : These are very important and should absolutely be covered in V2.

**Decimals**
- Understanding place value in decimals (tenths, hundredths)
- Comparing and ordering decimals
- Adding and subtracting decimals
- Multiplying decimals by 10, 100, 1000
- Connecting fractions and decimals (½ = 0.5, ¼ = 0.25)

Comment : Again crucial and should be covered in V2

**Probability — going deeper**
- Combined events (AND / OR)
- Listing outcomes systematically (tables, tree diagrams — introduction)
- Relative frequency and large samples
- Applied backgammon probability (feeds into Backgammon Lab)

**Statistics — going deeper**
- Stem-and-leaf plots
- Introduction to box plots (conceptual)
- Drawing conclusions from data: causation vs correlation (conceptual, intuitive)
- Surveys and sampling — what makes a good sample?

## V3 — 5th Grade

**Number & Operations**
- Prime numbers, composite numbers
- Prime factorisation (factor trees)
- Lowest Common Multiple (LCM) and Highest Common Factor (HCF/GCD)
- Order of operations — BODMAS / PEMDAS
- Negative numbers — introduction, number line, ordering
Comment : negative number can come much earlier, in V1 itself

- Adding and subtracting negative numbers
- Introduction to exponents (2³ = 8, square numbers, cube numbers)

**Fractions, Decimals, Percentages — the full triangle**
- Multiplying and dividing fractions (procedural fluency)
- Percentages — what they are, converting between fractions/decimals/percentages
- Percentage of a quantity (20% of 450)
- Percentage increase and decrease
- Ratio and proportion — introduction
- Scale factors

**Algebra**
- Simplifying algebraic expressions (collecting like terms)
- Expanding single brackets
- Solving two-step and multi-step equations
- Writing and solving equations from word problems
- Introduction to substitution (evaluate 3x + 2 when x = 5)
- Sequences — nth term (introduction)

**Geometry**
- Coordinates — four quadrants, plotting points, reading coordinates
- Translation, reflection, rotation (transformations)
- Volume of cuboids (l × w × h)
- Nets of 3D shapes
- Angles in parallel lines (corresponding, alternate — introduction)
- Circles — calculating circumference (C = πd)
- Circles — calculating area (A = πr²)
- Intersections of rectangles and computing areas of the intersection parts. Also - advanced : given two rectangles and their coordinates, can you determine IF they intersect or not?

**Statistics & Probability**
- Mean from a frequency table
- Pie charts — reading and drawing
- Two-way tables
- Probability: mutually exclusive events (P(A) + P(not A) = 1)
- Probability: combined events using tables and tree diagrams (procedural)
- Deeper into conditional probability
- Deeper into simple probability distributions and the intuition for why they are needed. Tie in a lot with real world

## V4 — 6th Grade and Beyond

> These are horizon topics — listed to show there is no ceiling, not to plan immediately.

**Number**
- Operations with negative numbers (all four)
- Standard form / scientific notation
- Irrational numbers — introduction (√2, π)
- Bounds and rounding (upper and lower bounds)

**Algebra**
- Factorising expressions
- Solving linear inequalities
- Simultaneous equations (two unknowns)
- Introduction to functions (input/output, function notation)
- Plotting linear graphs (y = mx + c)
- Gradient and intercept

**Geometry**
- Pythagoras' theorem
- Introduction to trigonometry (SOH CAH TOA)
- Circle theorems (introduction)
- Surface area of cylinders, cones, spheres
- Congruence and similarity

**Statistics & Probability**
- Histograms and cumulative frequency
- Stratified sampling
- Conditional probability (P(A|B))
- Permutations and combinations — introduction
- Expected value (formalising what she already knows from backgammon)

**Beyond — recreational mathematics (Gardner territory)**
- Graph theory — Seven Bridges of Königsberg
- Modular arithmetic and clock maths
- Fibonacci sequences and the golden ratio
- Introduction to proof (by example, by contradiction)
- Infinity — Hilbert's Hotel (conceptual)
- Cryptography — RSA at a conceptual level
